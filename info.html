Red Blood Cells (RBC)

  • Carry oxygen from the lungs to tissues and carbon dioxide back to the lungs.
  • Diseases: Anemia, Polycythemia.

Hemoglobin

  • Protein in RBCs that binds oxygen for transport.
  • Diseases: Sickle Cell Anemia, Thalassemia.

White Blood Cells (WBC)

  • Key role in the immune response.
  • Diseases: Leukopenia, Leukemia.

Neutrophils

  • First responders to microbial infection.
  • Diseases: Neutropenia, Chronic Granulomatous Disease.

Lymphocytes

  • Include T-cells, B-cells, and NK cells; vital for immunity.
  • Diseases: Lymphocytopenia, Lymphoma.

Eosinophils

  • Involved in allergic responses and parasitic infections.
  • Diseases: Eosinophilia, Eosinophilic Esophagitis.

Basophils

  • Release histamine during allergic reactions.
  • Diseases: Basophilia, Hypersensitivity Disorders.

Monocytes

  • Differentiate into macrophages and dendritic cells.
  • Diseases: Monocytosis, Monocytopenia.

Platelets

  • Essential for blood clotting.
  • Diseases: Thrombocytopenia, Thrombocytosis.

Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)

  • Measures the average size of RBCs.
  • Diseases: Microcytic Anemia, Macrocytic Anemia.
